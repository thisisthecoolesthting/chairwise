## Uplift V2 vs Fully Jarvis: Three Years Later

Standing desks have become a staple for healthy home and office setups, with two of the most talked-about models being the Uplift V2 and the Fully Jarvis. Both have strong followings, but how do they hold up after three years of use? In this article, we’ll take a pragmatic look at these two standing desks, focusing on durability, functionality, and overall value after extended real-world use — helping you decide which might still be the better long-term investment.

---

## Build Quality & Stability

### Uplift V2

The Uplift V2 has long been praised for its sturdy steel frame and solid construction. After three years, many users report that the frame remains rigid with little to no wobble, even at maximum height. The desk’s patented anti-collision system still performs reliably, minimizing accidental damage.

A notable feature is the dual-motor mechanism, which tends to maintain smooth and quiet operation over time. In comparison to some budget models, the Uplift’s build quality distinctly shows through with consistent performance and no significant mechanical wear reported.

### Fully Jarvis

The Fully Jarvis desk also uses a steel frame and dual-motor setup but typically features slightly different tube dimensions and lift mechanism tuning. After three years, users generally observe good durability and very minimal stability issues, but there have occasionally been reports of minor wobble at taller heights, particularly with heavier tops.

Jarvis’s motors are quiet and smooth but a handful of long-term users have noted potential slowdown or slightly increased noise with heavy use, though this is not a widespread problem. Overall, Jarvis remains highly regarded for stability in its class but might feel a tad less robust than the Uplift V2, especially under heavier loads.

---

## Height Range and Adjustment

### Uplift V2

The Uplift V2 impresses with its wide height range, suitable for a broad spectrum of users from seated to standing positions. Three years on, the height control mechanism remains responsive, and the programmable presets maintain their accuracy, allowing multiple users to switch ergonomic settings seamlessly.

This desk offers a low minimum height, which is appreciated by shorter users or those wanting a lower seated position. The speed of adjustment also remains consistent, with no noticeable slowing down over time.

### Fully Jarvis

Jarvis’s height range is similarly wide, although slightly less adjustable on the low end compared to Uplift V2. The ability to program and recall height presets continues to work well, and the motors provide swift transitions between heights.

Some users have noted that after prolonged use, the motors can get a little noisier, but this doesn’t generally impact function. The overall height range is sufficient for most users, but those requiring very low seating positions might find the Uplift’s lower minimum more accommodating.

---

## Desktop Options & Customization

### Uplift V2

One of Uplift’s strengths is the variety of desktop shapes, sizes, and materials available. This extensive customization remains a major selling point three years later as users can tailor their desk for specific needs — from large L-shaped desks to environmentally-friendly bamboo tops.

The quality of the desktop materials has generally held up well, resisting scratches and wear in everyday use. Users appreciate that the frame and desktop remain compatible with upgraded parts, meaning you don’t have to replace the entire setup if you want a new top or accessories later on.

### Fully Jarvis

Jarvis also offers a good selection of desktop options, although with fewer shapes than Uplift. Their tops are available in materials like bamboo and laminate, with solid durability for daily work.

Jarvis desks maintain their modularity over the years, allowing users to swap or upgrade the desktop if desired. However, some users find the customization options slightly more limited than Uplift’s, especially if looking for non-standard desk shapes or eco-focused materials.

---

## Accessories and Ecosystem

### Uplift V2

After three years, Uplift has developed a robust ecosystem of accessories specifically designed for their desks. This includes monitor arms, keyboard trays, wire management kits, and under-desk organizers.

Users find these enhance workflow and cable management, making the workspace cleaner. The compatibility and ease of installation of these accessories on the original desk frame remain a positive highlight.

### Fully Jarvis

Fully has also expanded its range of accessories, providing well-designed and functional add-ons like monitor arms, desk mats, and grommet cable management systems.

While not quite as extensive as Uplift’s ecosystem, Fully’s accessories are generally praised for seamless integration and good build quality. Long-term users appreciate that these upgrades can be added as needs evolve without requiring a new desk.

---

## Customer Support & Warranty Experience

### Uplift V2

Uplift’s customer support stands out for being reliable and responsive even years after purchase. Their warranty covers electronic components for several years, and many users report straightforward parts replacement and helpful guidance through troubleshooting.

The company has maintained consistent communication, providing firmware updates for the desk control pads that improve user experience. This kind of ongoing aftercare reinforces Uplift’s commitment to long-term product value.

### Fully Jarvis

Fully offers a similar warranty structure with solid customer support. Most users receive prompt attention to issues and the company honors warranty claims smoothly.

While generally positive, a handful of longer-term users note slower communication compared to Uplift in rare cases, though not enough to be a dealbreaker. The company also provides firmware support but with less frequency in updates.

---

## Price & Value After Three Years

Both Uplift V2 and Jarvis typically command a premium price at purchase, reflecting their quality and features. When considering the three-year mark, Uplift often retains slightly higher resale value due to its extensive customization options and reputation for durability.

Jarvis is typically priced a bit lower initially, offering great value for those who want a dependable desk without as many extras. Both desks are investments that tend to pay off in ergonomics and comfort, especially when considering health benefits over time.

---

## Final Thoughts: Which to Choose Three Years Later?

After three years of ownership, the decision between Uplift V2 and Fully Jarvis largely comes down to personal preferences and priorities:

- **Choose Uplift V2 if:**  
  You want the widest customization options, a slightly sturdier frame, a lower minimum height, and ongoing firmware and accessory support. It’s ideal if you value long-term adaptability and a broad ecosystem of upgrades.

- **Choose Fully Jarvis if:**  
  You prefer a reliable, slightly more affordable desk that delivers solid performance and stability with a clean design. Jarvis is a strong pick for those needing dependable height range without extra frills or the highest level of customization.

Both desks continue to be strong contenders even three years later, making them smart choices for ergonomic work setups. Your best pick depends on which features and long-term considerations matter most in your office environment.
